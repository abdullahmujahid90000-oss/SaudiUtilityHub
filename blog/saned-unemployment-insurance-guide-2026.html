SANED Unemployment Insurance Guide 2026 — Eligibility, Benefits & How to Apply

AA
Abdullah Al-Qahtani Founder & Editor, Saudi Utility Hub · Riyadh, Saudi Arabia
⚡ Quick Answer

SANED (ساند) is GOSI's unemployment insurance program for Saudi nationals in the private sector. It pays 60% of your average covered wage for the first 3 months, then 50% for up to 9 more months (12 months maximum per claim), capped at SAR 9,000/month. You must apply within 90 days of losing your job involuntarily, and you need at least 12 months of SANED contributions in the past 36 months to qualify. Expats are not covered.

What is SANED?

SANED is Saudi Arabia's unemployment insurance program, established by royal decree and launched by the General Organization for Social Insurance (GOSI) in 2014–2015. It exists to give Saudi private-sector workers a temporary, partial income if they lose their job through no fault of their own — bridging the gap between one job ending and the next one starting.

Alongside the monthly payment, SANED also connects claimants with job-search support and training programs through the Human Resources Development Fund (HRDF) and its Taqat employment portal, with the goal of helping people find new work rather than simply replacing lost income indefinitely.

⚠️ Saudi nationals only: SANED is funded by a dedicated 1.5% contribution split evenly between employer and employee (0.75% each) on top of the main GOSI pension contribution. Because non-Saudi employees and their employers do not pay into this branch, expatriate workers are not eligible for SANED under current law. If you are an expat concerned about income protection after job loss, your main entitlement is end-of-service gratuity, not SANED.

Who Is Eligible for SANED?

To qualify for a SANED payout, you generally need to meet all of the following conditions:

  • You are a Saudi national registered under the pension branch of the Social Insurance Law, and under 59 years of age.
  • You lost your job involuntarily — for example, your employer terminated your contract, made your role redundant, or the company closed. Resigning voluntarily, or being dismissed for a disciplinary reason attributable to you, generally disqualifies a claim.
  • You have contributed to SANED for at least 12 months within the past 36 months before becoming unemployed.
  • You are actively searching for work and registered with the Taqat (HRDF) employment portal.
  • You have no other income from a job, profession, or private business activity during the claim period.
  • You apply within 90 days of leaving your job.

How Much Does SANED Pay, and For How Long?

SANED calculates your payment as a percentage of your average GOSI-registered monthly wage (basic salary plus housing allowance) over roughly the past two years of contributions — not your final salary alone. The percentage steps down the longer you stay on the program:

Period Benefit Rate Maximum Monthly Payout
Months 1–3 60% of average covered wage SAR 9,000
Months 4–12 50% of average covered wage SAR 9,000

The maximum total duration is 12 months per claim, regardless of how long you remain unemployed. Payments stop earlier if you find new employment, since SANED is designed as a bridge — not a long-term income replacement.

💡 Worked example: A Saudi employee with an average covered wage of SAR 12,000/month who is laid off would receive roughly SAR 7,200/month (60%) for the first 3 months, then about SAR 6,000/month (50%) for up to 9 more months — well under the SAR 9,000 cap. Someone with a covered wage above SAR 15,000/month would instead be capped at the flat SAR 9,000 maximum during the 60% period.

How to Apply for SANED

  1. Check your eligibility on my.gov.sa Use the "Saned Eligibility" service: enter your national ID (civil registration) number and the verification code, then click Verify.
  2. Apply through your GOSI online account within 90 days Log in at gosi.gov.sa (or via Absher) and submit your SANED claim as soon as possible after your job ends — late applications risk losing eligibility.
  3. Register with Taqat (HRDF) You must be listed as actively job-seeking on the Taqat employment portal to remain eligible for ongoing payments.
  4. Confirm your bank details Payments are deposited directly into the bank account registered with GOSI/Absher — make sure it is current before applying.
  5. Keep reporting your job search SANED expects ongoing proof of active job-seeking and may require participation in HRDF training or job-matching programs to keep payments flowing.
⚠️ Don't miss the 90-day window: Applying late is one of the most common reasons claims are rejected. If you've just left a job involuntarily, check your SANED eligibility the same week — not after your severance runs out.

SANED vs. End-of-Service Gratuity — Not the Same Thing

SANED and end-of-service (EOS) gratuity are separate entitlements and are not mutually exclusive — an eligible Saudi employee who is laid off may be entitled to both: the one-time EOS gratuity from their employer under Article 84 of the Labor Law, and the ongoing monthly SANED payments from GOSI while job-searching. Common Questions About SANED

Are expats eligible for SANED unemployment insurance? ▼
No. SANED is exclusively for Saudi nationals working in the private sector (or government employees covered by the Social Insurance Law). Expatriate workers do not contribute to SANED and cannot claim it — their income protection typically comes from end-of-service gratuity instead.
How much does SANED pay per month? ▼
SANED pays 60% of your average covered monthly wage (based on your last 24 months of GOSI-registered wages) for the first 3 months of unemployment, then 50% for each month after that, up to a maximum of SAR 9,000 per month regardless of your actual salary.
How long can I receive SANED benefits? ▼
SANED pays for a maximum of 12 months per claim. If you find a new job before the 12 months are used up, payments stop once you start earning again.
What disqualifies someone from SANED? ▼
You are generally not eligible if you resigned voluntarily, were dismissed for a disciplinary reason attributable to you, have not contributed to SANED for at least 12 months within the past 36 months, are not actively searching for work, or do not register with the Taqat (HRDF) employment portal.
How do I check my SANED eligibility? ▼
Use the "Saned Eligibility" service on the my.gov.sa platform: enter your civil registration (national ID) number and the verification code, then click Verify. You can also check and apply directly through your GOSI online account within 90 days of leaving your job.
