NOC Letter Saudi Arabia 2026 — What It Is, When You Need It & Free English Template

A complete guide to the No Objection Certificate in Saudi Arabia: what it is, who needs it, exactly what it must contain, and a free English template you can hand to HR today.

Quick answer: An NOC (No Objection Certificate) is a formal letter issued by your employer on company letterhead confirming they have no objection to a specific action you want to take — such as applying for a bank loan, renting an apartment, or applying for a dependent visa. It is not required by Saudi Labour Law itself, but banks, landlords, and visa authorities routinely ask for one before processing your request.

What Is an NOC Letter in Saudi Arabia?

A No Objection Certificate (NOC) is a formal, written declaration issued by your employer — on official company letterhead with an authorised stamp — confirming two things: your current employment status, and the fact that the company has no objection to you taking a specific action. In Saudi Arabia, the term is used across banking, real estate, immigration, and education contexts, though the underlying document is always the same in structure.

It is important to understand what an NOC is not:

  • Not a salary certificate — a salary certificate confirms your monthly income and is used primarily for loan eligibility calculations. An NOC confirms consent to an action.
  • Not an experience letter — an experience letter summarises your role history and is typically issued on leaving a company. An NOC is issued while you are still employed.
  • Not an employment contract — your contract governs the legal terms of employment. An NOC is a standalone, purpose-specific letter that references your contract details but has no contractual force of its own.

As a practical matter, the employee requests the NOC but the employer is the issuing party. The employer retains full discretion over whether to issue one, unless the employment contract or company policy says otherwise.

SAMA (the Saudi Central Bank) does not mandate the NOC specifically, but each licensed bank sets its own documentation requirements and most have standardised this practice.

What Must an NOC Contain?

While there is no single official Saudi government format for an NOC, all accepted letters share the same core elements. Missing any of these typically results in the letter being rejected and the employee having to go back to HR for a corrected version.

  1. Company letterhead with official stamp — the letter must be on the company's printed stationery and carry the company's official rubber stamp (seal). A typed company name alone is not sufficient.
  2. Date of issue — clearly stated at the top of the letter. Most authorities consider an NOC valid for three months from this date.
  3. Employee full name — exactly as it appears on the employee's Iqama or national ID.
  4. Iqama number (for expatriates) or National ID number (for Saudi nationals).
  5. Job title and department — the employee's current position and the department or business unit they belong to.
  6. Monthly salary — required by most banks and finance companies; some versions omit it for non-financial purposes such as visa applications.
  7. Length of service — the start date of employment and total duration to date. This helps the receiving party assess stability.
  8. Specific statement of no objection — the core purpose of the letter. This must name the exact action, for example: "We have no objection to the above-named employee applying for a personal finance product with [Bank Name]." A generic "no objection to whatever they may need" statement is often rejected.
  9. Authorised signatory name, title, and signature — the letter must be signed by an authorised representative of the company, typically the HR Manager, General Manager, or a named director. The signatory's full name and title must be printed below the signature.

Step-by-Step: How to Request an NOC from Your Employer

  1. Identify the exact purpose — before approaching HR, confirm with the bank, landlord, or authority exactly what wording they need on the NOC. Some institutions (especially banks) have a printed form they want filled in on letterhead rather than a free-form letter.
  2. Submit a written request to HR — email or use your company's HR portal. State the purpose, the name of the institution you are submitting to, any specific wording requirements, and the deadline you need it by.
  3. Allow 3–5 working days — most HR departments in Saudi Arabia process NOC requests within this window. If you need it urgently, say so in your request and follow up by phone or in person after 24 hours.
  4. Review the letter carefully before submitting it — check your name against your Iqama, confirm the Iqama number is correct, and verify the salary figure if included. Errors at this stage cost time and may require a second HR request.
  5. Keep a signed copy for your own records — you may need the same NOC again, or a future employer may ask for evidence of your previous employment documentation history.
  6. If your employer refuses — ask for the refusal in writing, citing the specific reason. If your contract or company policy entitles you to documentation support and the employer refuses without valid reason, you may be able to raise a formal complaint via the Qiwa platform or the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development (HRSD) at hrsd.gov.sa.
✅ Tip: NOC letters are usually valid for 3 months from the date of issue. Always confirm the validity window with the bank or authority you are submitting to before requesting the letter — if you request it too early, it may expire before your application is processed.

What If Your Employer Refuses to Issue an NOC?

⚠️ Important: Employers in Saudi Arabia are not legally obligated to issue an NOC in most circumstances — it is a discretionary act. However, if your employment contract or your company's HR policy contains a clause guaranteeing you documentation support, a refusal may constitute a breach of contract.

If your employer declines to issue an NOC, take the following practical steps:

  • Ask for the reason in writing — a verbal refusal is difficult to act on. Request that HR confirm the refusal and the reason via email.
  • Escalate to management — if HR refuses, escalate to the HR Director, Country Manager, or relevant senior officer and repeat the written request.
  • Check whether the requesting party will accept alternatives — some banks and landlords will accept a salary certificate plus a copy of your employment contract as a substitute if you can demonstrate that the employer is uncooperative. Ask explicitly.
  • File a complaint via Qiwa — for expatriate employees, the Qiwa platform (qiwa.com.sa) allows you to raise labour-related complaints directly with HRSD. For Saudi nationals, the same route applies. A formal complaint log may prompt an employer to reconsider.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is an NOC letter mandatory in Saudi Arabia? ▼
An NOC is not required by the Saudi Labour Law itself, but banks, landlords, visa authorities, and other third parties often require one before approving a request. Whether you need one depends entirely on what the institution you are dealing with asks for.
How long is an NOC letter valid in Saudi Arabia? ▼
Most NOC letters are valid for 3 months from the date of issue. Always check the validity requirements with the requesting party before asking HR to prepare the letter — requesting it too early means it may expire before your application is processed.
Can my employer charge me for issuing an NOC? ▼
No. An employer cannot legally charge an employee for issuing an NOC or any other standard employment-related document. If you are asked to pay, report it to HRSD via hrsd.gov.sa or raise a complaint on the Qiwa platform.
Is an NOC the same as a salary certificate? ▼
No. A salary certificate confirms your monthly gross salary and employment status — it is used primarily for loan eligibility calculations. An NOC confirms that the company has no objection to a specific action you intend to take. Banks in Saudi Arabia typically require both documents when processing a personal finance application.
Can an employer refuse to issue an NOC? ▼
Employers are generally not legally obligated to issue an NOC — it is a discretionary act. However, if your employment contract or company HR policy includes a provision for documentation support, refusal may constitute a breach. In that case, ask for the refusal in writing, escalate internally, and if necessary file a complaint via the Qiwa platform or HRSD.
Does the NOC need to be in Arabic? ▼
For Saudi government authorities such as Jawazat or HRSD, an Arabic NOC (or a certified Arabic translation) is usually preferred. For banks and international institutions, an English NOC on company letterhead with an official stamp is typically accepted. When in doubt, ask the receiving party before requesting the letter from HR.
