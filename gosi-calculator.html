In Saudi Arabia 2026, the GOSI base is basic salary plus housing allowance, capped at SAR 45,000. Saudi nationals already registered with GOSI before July 3, 2024 stay on the original rate: employee 9.75% (max SAR 4,387.50), employer 11.75% (max SAR 5,287.50) — a combined 21.5%. Saudi nationals newly registered on or after July 3, 2024 are on a staged schedule under the New Social Insurance System: 10.75% employee / 12.75% employer in 2026, rising toward 11.75%/13.75% by 2028. Expat (non-Saudi) employees pay 0%; their employer pays only a 2% occupational-hazard contribution (max SAR 900), never deducted from the employee's salary. 🆕 Two GOSI Tracks Since July 2024: Existing Subscribers vs. New Entrants

Saudi Arabia's New Social Insurance System (Royal Decree M/273) took effect on July 3, 2024, creating two parallel contribution tracks for Saudi nationals. Expat employees are not affected — their employer-only 2% occupational-hazard rate stays the same either way.

TrackEmployee RateEmployer Rate
Existing subscriber (GOSI contributions before July 3, 2024)9.75%11.75%
New entrant — 202510.25%12.25%
New entrant — 2026 (current)10.75%12.75%
New entrant — 2027 (planned)11.25%13.25%
New entrant — 2028 (planned)11.75%13.75%

"New entrant" means a Saudi national registered with GOSI for the first time on or after July 3, 2024. If you had any GOSI contribution history before that date, you stay on the original 9.75%/11.75% rate regardless of when you later change jobs. If you're unsure which track applies to you, check your registration date on the Qiwa portal or GOSI mobile app — this calculator cannot look up your registration date for you.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the GOSI employee contribution rate in 2026?
As of 2026, the GOSI employee contribution rate for Saudi nationals depends on registration track. Existing subscribers (registered before July 3, 2024) pay 9.75% of basic salary plus housing allowance, capped at SAR 45,000. New entrants (first registered on or after July 3, 2024) pay a staged rate that reaches 10.75% in 2026, rising toward 11.75% by 2028. Expat (non-Saudi) employees do not pay any employee GOSI contribution.
What is the GOSI employer contribution rate?
For Saudi national employees, employers contribute 11.75% of basic salary plus housing allowance for existing subscribers (registered before July 3, 2024), or a staged rate reaching 12.75% in 2026 for new entrants (registered on or after July 3, 2024) — both capped at SAR 45,000. For expat employees, the employer pays only 2% occupational-hazard insurance on the same base, unaffected by the new-entrant schedule.
What's the difference between an existing GOSI subscriber and a new entrant?
This distinction comes from Saudi Arabia's New Social Insurance System (Royal Decree M/273), effective July 3, 2024. If you had any GOSI contribution history before that date, you are an existing subscriber and stay on the original 9.75%/11.75% rate. If your first-ever GOSI registration was on or after July 3, 2024, you are a new entrant and your pension contribution rate rises annually — 10.25%/12.25% in 2025, up to a planned 11.75%/13.75% by 2028 — while the SANED (0.75%) and occupational-hazard (2% employer) portions stay the same either way. Check your registration date on the Qiwa portal or GOSI app if you're unsure which track applies to you.
Does GOSI apply to expatriate workers?
Expat (non-Saudi) employees do NOT pay an employee GOSI deduction. Only Saudi nationals pay the 9.75% employee contribution. For expats, the employer pays a 2% occupational-hazard contribution, which is not deducted from the employee's salary.
Is housing allowance included in GOSI calculation?
Yes. The GOSI base is basic salary PLUS housing allowance (capped at SAR 45,000). Transport, food, and other allowances are not included in the GOSI base.
What is the maximum GOSI deduction?
For a Saudi national, the maximum employee GOSI deduction is SAR 4,387.50 (9.75% of SAR 45,000) and the maximum employer contribution is SAR 5,287.50 (11.75% of SAR 45,000). For an expat employee there is no employee deduction; the employer's occupational-hazard maximum is SAR 900 (2% of SAR 45,000).
How to check GOSI contributions?
Employees can check their GOSI contributions through the Qiwa portal (qiwa.sa) or the GOSI mobile app.
Can I get a GOSI refund?
Expatriate workers may be eligible for a GOSI refund upon final exit from Saudi Arabia, provided they meet eligibility criteria.
What happens if my employer doesn't pay GOSI?
You can report non-compliance to the Ministry of Human Resources via the Qiwa platform or Absher.
