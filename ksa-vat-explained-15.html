Saudi Arabia VAT — Complete Guide 2026

Saudi Arabia introduced Value Added Tax (VAT / ضريبة القيمة المضافة) on January 1, 2018 at a rate of 5%. In response to the COVID-19 pandemic's economic impact, the rate was tripled to 15% on July 1, 2020.

Who Must Register for VAT?

  • Businesses with annual taxable revenue exceeding SAR 375,000 must register
  • Businesses between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000 may register voluntarily
  • Foreign businesses supplying taxable goods/services to Saudi Arabia

What is Subject to 15% VAT?

Most goods and services in Saudi Arabia are subject to the standard 15% VAT rate, including electronics, clothing, restaurants, hotels, professional services, and construction.

VAT-Exempt and Zero-Rated Items

  • Most basic food items and vegetables
  • Healthcare services and medicines
  • Education services (accredited institutions)
  • Residential rent (first 3 years)
  • Exported goods and international services
  • Financial services (specific types)
  • Life insurance and reinsurance

Real Estate and VAT

As of October 2020, real estate transfers are subject to a 15% Real Estate Transaction Tax (RETT) instead of VAT. First-time Saudi homebuyers can claim a government rebate on RETT up to SAR 1 million on the property value.

Tourist VAT Refunds

Tourists visiting Saudi Arabia can claim a 15% VAT refund on eligible purchases at departure airports. Use the Planet tax refund service with your original receipts. Minimum purchase SAR 200 per receipt.

How VAT Is Calculated — Worked Example

VAT in Saudi Arabia is calculated by multiplying the price before tax by 15% (0.15). If a product costs SAR 1,000 before tax, the VAT owed is SAR 150, making the final price SAR 1,150. To work backwards from a VAT-inclusive price, divide by 1.15 to find the base price, then subtract that from the total to find the VAT portion.

  • Adding VAT: Price × 1.15 = Total with VAT (e.g. SAR 500 × 1.15 = SAR 575)
  • Removing VAT: Total ÷ 1.15 = Price before VAT (e.g. SAR 575 ÷ 1.15 = SAR 500)
  • VAT amount only: Total − (Total ÷ 1.15) = VAT portion (e.g. SAR 575 − SAR 500 = SAR 75)

ZATCA E-Invoicing (Fatoora) — What Businesses Must Do

Since December 2021, ZATCA has rolled out mandatory e-invoicing (Fatoora) in two phases. Phase 1 (Generation), effective since December 2021, requires all VAT-registered businesses to generate invoices electronically with a QR code, rather than handwritten or basic PDF invoices. Phase 2 (Integration), rolled out in waves by taxpayer size since 2023 and continuing through 2026, requires invoicing systems to integrate directly with ZATCA's platform for real-time clearance and reporting of B2B invoices, and simplified reporting for B2C receipts.

  • Non-compliant invoicing systems can trigger fines starting at SAR 5,000 for first violations, rising for repeat non-compliance
  • Simplified tax invoices (for retail/B2C) must include a QR code readable by ZATCA's verification app
  • Standard tax invoices (for B2B) must include the buyer's VAT number if they are also VAT-registered

VAT Return Filing Deadlines & Penalties

VAT-registered businesses must file returns and pay any VAT owed by the deadlines below. Missing a deadline results in automatic penalties, separate from any VAT owed.

  • Monthly filers (annual revenue above SAR 40 million): return due by the last day of the month following the tax period
  • Quarterly filers (annual revenue below SAR 40 million): return due by the last day of the month following the quarter's end
  • Late filing penalty: 5% to 25% of the unpaid tax, depending on how late the filing is
  • Late payment penalty: 5% of unpaid tax for each 30 days it remains outstanding
  • Failure to register when required: a fixed penalty of SAR 10,000

VAT for Freelancers & Small Business (Freelance Licence Holders)

Freelancers operating under a Saudi Freelance Licence (Muqawil individual licence) are subject to the same registration threshold as any other business: registration becomes mandatory once annual taxable income exceeds SAR 375,000, and is optional between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000. Below SAR 187,500, no VAT registration or filing is required. Many freelancers voluntarily register below the mandatory threshold so they can reclaim VAT paid on business expenses (input VAT), such as software subscriptions, equipment, or co-working space rent.

How Saudi's 15% VAT Compares to Neighbouring Gulf Countries

  • Saudi Arabia: 15% (raised from 5% in July 2020)
  • UAE: 5% (unchanged since introduction in 2018)
  • Bahrain: 10%
  • Oman: 5%
  • Qatar & Kuwait: VAT not yet implemented as of 2026

Saudi Arabia currently has the highest standard VAT rate in the GCC, reflecting the government's push to diversify state revenue away from oil under Vision 2030.

Frequently Asked Questions

When was Saudi VAT introduced?▾
VAT was introduced at 5% on January 1, 2018, and raised to 15% on July 1, 2020 to support government revenues during COVID-19.
Are supermarket groceries subject to VAT in Saudi Arabia?▾
Basic food items (staples, vegetables, dairy) are generally zero-rated. However, prepared foods, snacks, soft drinks, and restaurant meals are taxable at 15%.
How do I get a VAT invoice in Saudi Arabia?▾
Businesses registered for VAT must issue tax invoices for taxable sales. The invoice must include the ZATCA VAT number, tax amount, and QR code for simplified invoices.
What happens if I file my VAT return late?▾
Late filing incurs a penalty of 5% to 25% of the unpaid tax depending on the delay, plus a separate 5% penalty for every 30 days the payment remains outstanding.
Do freelancers in Saudi Arabia need to register for VAT?▾
Only if annual taxable income exceeds SAR 375,000 (mandatory) or is between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000 (optional). Below SAR 187,500, no registration is required, though voluntary registration can allow reclaiming input VAT on business expenses.
Is Saudi Arabia's VAT rate the highest in the Gulf?▾
Yes. At 15%, Saudi Arabia has the highest standard VAT rate in the GCC, compared to 5% in the UAE and Oman, and 10% in Bahrain. Qatar and Kuwait had not implemented VAT as of 2026.
