📖 Understanding Gold Prices in Saudi Arabia

Gold prices in Saudi Arabia fluctuate daily based on global market trends, the US Dollar exchange rate, and local demand. The reference prices on this page are for raw gold only — when purchasing jewelry, you'll also pay making charges (maksariya) that vary by store, plus VAT on the making-charge component.

Key Facts About Gold Karats

  • 24 Karat (99.9% pure): Best for investment (bars, coins). Minimal making charges.
  • 22 Karat (91.7% pure): Most common for traditional Saudi jewelry. Higher making charges.
  • 21 Karat (87.5% pure): Popular for bridal sets and heavy jewelry.
  • 18 Karat (75% pure): Used for modern, lightweight designs. More durable than higher karats.

💱 How the Buy/Sell Spread Works

Gold dealers rarely buy and sell at the exact same price. Just like currency exchange counters, they quote two numbers: a higher price to sell you gold, and a lower price at which they'll buy it back from you. In Saudi Arabia, this spread is commonly around 40 to 50 SAR per gram, though it can vary depending on the dealer, the city, whether the gold is 24K bullion or finished jewelry, and how competitive the local market is.

This spread exists because dealers take on costs and risk: verifying purity, covering overheads, insuring stock, and making a margin on every transaction. It is completely normal and not unique to Saudi Arabia — you'll see similar spreads in gold markets worldwide.

The calculator above models this by treating the live market price as the approximate selling price, and adding your chosen spread (default 45 SAR/gram) to estimate the buying price. If your dealer quotes a different spread, simply move the slider to match it.

🏪 Where to Buy or Sell Gold in Saudi Arabia

Major gold souqs include:

  • Riyadh: Deira Gold Souq, Olaya Street
  • Jeddah: Al-Balad Historic District, Tahlia Street
  • Dammam: King Fahd Road, Dhahran Mall area

You can also buy investment-grade gold from banks like Al Rajhi Bank and SNB. When you're ready to sell, most licensed dealers will buy back gold they didn't sell you too — but keep in mind:

  • Bring the original invoice or purity certificate if you have one; it can help you get a better rate and proves authenticity.
  • Dealers will typically weigh and test the gold on the spot (using an XRF tester or acid test) before quoting a buy-back price.
  • Jewelry with stones, engravings, or heavy wear may be valued only for its gold content, not its craftsmanship.
  • Getting quotes from 2-3 dealers before selling is a simple way to make sure you're getting a fair, competitive spread.

🧾 Do You Pay VAT on Gold in Saudi Arabia?

Saudi Arabia's standard VAT rate is 15%. Under ZATCA's regulations, investment-grade gold — generally 99% purity or higher, in the form of bars, ingots, or coins recognized by bullion markets — is normally VAT-exempt when the conditions for investment metals are met. Gold jewelry, however, is typically treated differently: VAT is usually applied to the making-charge (labor) portion of the price, though treatment can vary by retailer and invoice structure. This calculator does not add VAT to its estimates, so always confirm the exact tax treatment with your dealer or check ZATCA's guidance before a large purchase.

✅ Tips Before You Buy or Sell Gold

  • Compare the live spot-based reference price on this page with what a dealer is quoting — a huge gap may signal an unfair spread.
  • Ask whether the quoted price includes making charges, VAT, and any certification fee.
  • For investment purposes, 24K bars or coins usually carry the lowest making charges and the tightest buy/sell spread.
  • Keep receipts and certificates — they make future resale faster and can support a better buy-back price.
  • Check the live chart above before a large transaction to see whether gold has been trending up or down recently.
  • Prices move throughout the trading day — refresh this page for the latest rate before finalizing a deal.

Why is the buying price higher than the selling price?
Gold dealers and jewelers add a margin, usually around 40-50 SAR per gram in Saudi Arabia, between what they charge you to buy gold and what they pay you to buy it back. This spread covers their operating costs, handling, certification, and profit margin. It is normal and applies across most gold souqs and jewelers.
How is the buy/sell spread calculated on this calculator?
The calculator treats the live market price as the estimated selling price, and adds a dealer premium (default 45 SAR/gram, adjustable between 40 and 50 SAR) to get the estimated buying price. You can move the slider to match the spread quoted by your local dealer.
How often do gold prices change in KSA?
Gold prices in Saudi Arabia change multiple times per day based on global market movements and local demand. This page pulls the latest spot price on every visit.
What is the difference between gold karats?
24K is 99.9% pure gold (best for investment), 22K is 91.7% pure (common for jewelry), 21K is 87.5% pure (traditional designs), 18K is 75% pure (modern jewelry).
Where can I buy or sell gold in Saudi Arabia?
You can buy or sell gold at licensed dealers in major gold souqs in Riyadh (Deira), Jeddah (Al-Balad), and Dammam, or through banks like Al Rajhi Bank and SNB that offer investment gold. When selling, dealers typically ask for the original invoice or a certificate of purity.
Is the price shown with or without making charges?

Does this calculator include VAT?

This calculator does not add VAT — confirm the exact treatment with your dealer or ZATCA before you buy.
